GA-AVATAR: GEOMETRY-APPEARANCE DECOUPLED HUMAN AVATAR MODELING BASED ON GAUSSIAN SPLATTING

Paper arXiv Code

GA-Avatar Framework

Abstract

The challenge of 3D human avatar modeling from monocular video is how to maintain the geometric consistency and realistic appearance of the avatar under limited observations and complex poses. Existing Gaussian-based methods usually rely on human body templates for Gaussian initialization, which lack geometric optimization and constraints, resulting in structural drift and blurred details in novel poses. To address above problems, we propose GA-Avatar, a dual-branch reconstruction network via decoupled geometry and appearance modeling, which improves the geometric accuracy as well as rendering quality. First, we construct a high-resolution mesh in canonical space, bind Gaussian primitives to mesh vertices, and achieve personalized geometric adjustments by optimizing Gaussian positions. Based on canonical features of the Gaussians, we design a dual-branch modeling network for geometry and appearance, learning vertex deformation and color features, respectively. To improve the modeling capabilities of non-rigid deformations, we introduce pose-dependent deformation modules in both the geometry and color branches. Based on the high-quality canonical avatar, we apply linear blend skinning and differentiable rendering to obtain the posed human avatar. In the optimization stage, in addition to conventional photometric loss, we proposed a geometric optimization strategy based on depth and normal constraints, which effectively enhanced the structure preservation and appearance consistency of the model in different poses. We conduct rich experiments and comparisons, and GA-Avatar outperforms existing methods on high-fidelity rendering on multiple datasets.

Index Terms: Human Avatar Modeling, Gaussian Splatting, 3D Reconstruction
